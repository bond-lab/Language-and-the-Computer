LAC: Language and the Computer: Project 2

This assignment constitutes 30% of your final grade for LAC. Please work on this in groups of two or three.

Pick one of the following:

A: Consistency in Sentiment Lexicons (monolingual)

You have been given some sentiment data from the NTU-MC corpus and a function to read in sentiment scores (between -1.0 and 1.0 in wordnet). Compare it to word level sentiment lexicons from other projects (and those projects to each other).

You can use the wordnet sentiment data from Project 1.

There is a some scaffolding to help you get started with the English data: compare-senti.py (formatted, output), and here is the data it needs en_senti.zip. Put the data in the same directory as the program. Please feel free to extend this (or start from scratch).

And here is the data individually:

  • DAL
  • AFINN
  • GLAS
  • VADER

Minimal Requirements

  1. Compare at least three of these resources with each other and with the NTU-MC corpus data.
    1. AFINN sentiment data Nielsen (2011)
    2. Dictionary of Affect in Language (DAL) Whissel (2000)
    3. Glasgow Norms Scott et al (2019)
    4. Vader Sentiment C.J. Hutto and Eric Gilbert (2014)
  2. How well do the lexicons compare in terms of consistency and coverage?
    are there consistent differences?
  3. How do they compare with the NTU-MC wordnet data?
  4. Consider especially the problem of different senses having different sentiment: the Glasgow Norms have some data sense differentiated which may be useful for this.

Stretch Goals

  1. Try to merge the lexicons into one bigger, better lexicon, and explain why you think it is better
  2. Find other lexicons to compare
  3. Can the word level lexicons be used to extend the wordnet lexicon? If so, how?
  4. DAL and the Glasgow Norms have scores on other dimensions (such as Activation and Imagery) compare them as well
  5. Look at the effect of morphology (verb inflections, comparatives and superlatives, derivational linsk)
  6. Show how you can extend/check the scores

B: Consistency in Sentiment Lexicons (multilingual)

Look for sentiment lexicons in other languages, and use the multilingual wordnet to compare values.

This is more difficult than A as you have to deal with translation shift as well as word/sense and resource quality issues.

C: Own Task

You can suggest your own task, and if I say ok, do it instead. This can be used to fit in with other research you are doing, but should not duplicate work done for other assessment, you must do something new for this class.

Deliverable

You should deliver a paper, the program and the output files.

  • The paper should be no more than ten pages including diagrams, with up to two additional pages of references,
  • You should give concrete examples from the resource(s) you analyzed.
  • Include quantitive results.
  • Include representative examples.
  • You don't need an extensive literature review, but you should read and cite the references below and if you consult other lexicons (which you are encouraged to do) then you should read the papers that describe them carefully, and cite them.
  • Formatted according to the LMS guidelines to submitting written work for the Division of LMS (but see below).
    • You do not have to follow the suggested structure of "Introduction, Literature Review, Methodology, Results, Discussion, Conclusion, References." A short introduction describing the task followed by Results, Discussion, Conclusion, References is enough.
    • You should use single spacing, not double spacing.
  • Include your entire program (including internal documentation) as an appendix: this does not count toward the ten page total.
    In the main paper describe generally what the program does (no need to go into extreme detail)
  • If you want to make it even more beautiful, as I am sure you do, take a look at my (Computational) Linguistic Style Guidelines: a guide for the flummoxed.
  • Submit softcopy (via NTULearn).
  • The deadline is on the main page..
