Visualization

Present nicely a tagged corpus of the form: 

    [(sid, wid, word, pos, cid, clemma, tag, sentiment, comment)+]
  
    The dog barked.
    [(1, 1, 'The' , 'DT', 'the', '', '', '', 0.0, ''),
    (1, 2, 'dog' , 'NNS', 'dog', 1, 'the', '02084071-n', 0.0, ''),
    (1, 3, 'barked', 'VBD', 'bark', 2, 'the',  '01047745-v', 0.0, ''),
    (1, 4, '.', '.', '.', None, 0.0, '')]

You should first download:

  • sents.json the sentences
  • words.json the tagged words
  • p3.py some code to start you off
    Running this produces a file p3.html
  • You may have to install the Wn module

Richard Brath has a nice discussion of Potential uses of font attributes in visualization.

I am most interested in seeing the sentiment

  • You can show polarity in color
    I am happy .
    I am unhappy .
  • You can show strength in size
    I am happy .
    I am ecstatic .
  • You can show strength in weight
    I am happy .
    I am ecstatic .
  • You could try to show the whole sentence, or paragraph, ...
    I am happy .
    I am ecstatic .
  • You could try to show negators or operators
    I am not happy .
    I am very happy .
    I am very happy .

Try to do some extra calculation before you show things

  • use the structure of wordnet to, e.g. identify negators or operators
    • or look for word lists (and reference them)
  • calculate the sentence polarity (even just summing the polarity)
  • calculate the paragraph polarity (even just summing the polarity)

You could also show other things:

  • Make different POS different fonts (or colors)

  • Show frequency with size

  • Augment words with translations using a tooltip
    The dog barked .
